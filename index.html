Taste the Feeling

In 1886, pharmacist John Pemberton invented this drink as a treatment for morphine addiction, introduced Coca Cola to a large pharmacy: consisting of water, sugar, caffeine, coca leaf extracts and cola nut, where the drink was sold at 5 cents per glass. In 1888, Pemberton died in poverty, and the rights were bought from his widow by entrepreneur Eiza Candler for $ 2300, who in 1892 registered this company under the name Coca-Cola Company and in ten years, together with 50 employees, created the national brand Coca-Cola. In 1899, the first franchise company was founded to produce this drink. This model, in which the parent company limited itself to the production of beverage concentrates and their marketing, and third-party companies bought these concentrates, diluted them with sweetened carbonated water, bottled and sold them, persisted for most of the 20th century.
